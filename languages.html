Let's Talk!

Languages open new doors

I started learning languages as a child. In the '80s, when Poland was a part of the Soviet Block, we often had contacts with people from other countries of the Soviet Union. In these times, everybody knew russian, so my parents sometimes used a russian word, here and there, as a play. Learning russian was not so much "learning", but it was a game, something fun.


In the '90s, after the fall of the Soviet Union, things changed. Instead of russian, schools started to teach other languages, like english, german, spanish. We sang songs in english just like we heard them in the radio, and there was nobody yet to correct us.


When I was a teenager, I got a high score at math competition called "Kangaroo". As I prize, I went to a math camp with other teenagers from all around the Europe, also laureates of the contest. At that point I understood the importance of knowing other languages. We were only able to communicate with in each other on a very basic level, but we were not afraid to learn.
